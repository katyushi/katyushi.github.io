    My Experience at the 61º LIYSF

    by Hugo lazzariSept 25th, 2019

    In the picture its me and my teacher lia a.k.a. lady lia in front of the buckinghan palace a couple of hours before going back to brazil after LIYSF. 

    So what is LYISF and why i went to

    Once a year in london there's a event called London International Youth Science Forum. Founded in 1959, LIYSF aims to give a deeper insight into science and its applications for the benefit of all mankind and to develop a greater understanding between young people of all nations.

    I was invited to participate because of my teacher saw potential in me and wanted me to participate in the forum so i begun to make some projects to apply and i came up with FirstAid an app to serve as a quick guide for situations that may need first aid knowledge. and i helped a colleague with his idea Ecoleta an app to help people teach people how to recicle. i ended presenting at the science bazaar only Ecoleta because he couldn't travel

    while i was there i made a lot of friends from brazil and another countries around the world. it was fantastic to exchange ideas and talk to people from another countries and my country as well. i saw a lot of awesome ideas at the science bazaar on LIYSF and exchanged some ideas about projects an incredible networking experience.

    so if you have an good idea for a project that may help humanity i recomend you give LIYSF a try to apply you have to:

    • Be Aged between 16 and 21 years old when LIYSF takes place
    • Students must have a good understanding of written and spoken English
    • Students must be studying science and have achieved or be in preparation for taking exam qualifications for university entrance

    you can see more about how to apply here: https://www.liysf.org.uk/liysf-participation/booking-forms more information in general you can find at:  https://www.liysf.org.uk/
